Deciding to backtrack, you set off towards the woods. As you walk, you start planning the best corse of action. On one hand: if your friends woke up as dazed as you did, they may have wandered into the woods alone. With night closing in their chances of survival lessen by the minute. By looking for footprints or other signs of life you may be able to find them. On the other hand, if you go out into the woods alone you may not fare any better than them. You could follow the road back and try and find a phone to call for help.

Bypassing the quickly darkening forest, you decide to stick along the road and see if you can get somewhere with a phone. Mysterious disappearances are for professionals to solve. As you walk along the unusually desolate road you notice a bright light creeping over your shoulder, illuminating the path in front of you. You turn to see a van driving in your direction. The driver seems to notice you too as he slows beside you and rolls down his window. You explain your circumstances and he offers you a ride to a nearby gas station. Do you accept his offer?

The thought of your friends dying in the woods is too much to bear. You decide the risk is worth it and head off into the woods. As you walk the woods get progressively darker until your eyes become almost completely useless. When you’re relying on your hearing, the sounds of the woods are magnified tenfold. Crickets chirping, owls hooting, muffled voices, leaves rustling... ...Muffled voices?

Fearful as to what else is in these woods, you begin running full-speed away from the mumbling. Unable to see in the dark of night, you have to rely on instinct as a navigator; one wrong turn and the woods will swallow you whole.
